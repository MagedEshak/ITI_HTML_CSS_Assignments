Concept of the Oud machine:

Oud is known as a musical instrument with a deep box shape, a finger clickpad but no orchards, which appeared in medieval music and modern Islamic music.

The sounds are based on the play blade clicking on the fixed and tight machine chords in the machine rake, the tone adjustment switches appear on both sides of the key box, and the common traditional and common judo consists of four pairs of strings, but there are types of five or six pairs.

The history of the Oud machine:

Many historians and the people's music scientists believe that the Oud machine appeared in the old Egypt more than 3,500 years ago, and the date of the appearance of the long Oud date back to the year 1,300 BC.

Historians found a piece believed to be a feather - made of wood - fixed on the Oud by a rope in the cargo cemetery in one of Pharaoh's temples, and shows the Egyptian society's interest in music by honoring music, where they made it a God called Hathor.

Many researchers showed an investigation into the origin of the Oud according to the evidence they found, including what the researcher Kathleen Schlesinger believed that Oud was born in Persia, and then moved to the Arabs by the end of the sixth century.
